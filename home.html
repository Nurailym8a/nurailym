Morandi's Vision of Bologna

Giorgio Morandi (1890-1964) was one of the greatest Italian artists of the early 20th century. Born and raised in Bologna, Morandi spent almost his entire life in this historic city, drawing profound inspiration from its rich cultural heritage and tranquil surroundings.

His early works were influenced by Cézanne and the Italian Renaissance, but he gradually developed a unique style characterized by muted colors and subtle compositions. The serene landscapes and quiet charm of Bologna deeply influenced Morandi’s work, reflecting the city’s timeless beauty and harmonious balance.

Morandi’s legacy is a testament to Bologna’s vibrant artistic heritage, positioning the city as a nurturing ground for creativity and innovation.

Our Project

The exploration of Giorgio Morandi’s paintings, particularly "Paesaggio. Grizzana" and "View of the Montagnola of Bologna, Glimpse of a Garden," provides a fascinating opportunity to blend art history with advanced data technologies.

Our project focuses on three main goals: creating precise SPARQL queries to extract relevant data, leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) to enrich our understanding, and generating new knowledge triples to enhance the ArCo knowledge graph. By systematically combining these methodologies, we aim to deepen the contextual and historical understanding of Morandi’s works, demonstrating the powerful intersection of traditional art research and modern computational tools.
